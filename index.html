Classical Dances Of India

Bharatnatayam
It is a dance of Tamil Nadu.
It traces its origins back to the
Natyashastra, an ancient treatise on
theatre written by Bharata.

Kathak
It originated from Uttar Pradesh.
This one is derived from the word Katha
meaning story, and during the
whole dance, the dancers narrate
stories through their eyes and expressions.

Kathakali
It comes from southwestern India,
around the state of Kerala.
It combines devotion, drama, dance,
music, costumes and make up into a divine
experience for all who get to view it.

Kuchipudi
It originates from a village named
Kuchipudi in Andhra Pradesh. Kuchipudi
is a dance-drama performance, with
its roots in the Natya Shastra.

Manipuri
It is an ancient classical dance form
that originated in Manipur.
Its origins can be traced back to ancient
times, even before recorded history began.

Mohiniyattam
It is an Indian classical dance form originating from Kerala.
The dance gets its name from Mohini – the
female enchantress avatar of the lord Vishnu.

Odissi
It originates from Odisha.
According to Natya Shastra, 'Odhra Magadha'
is the earliest form of present-day Odissi.

Sattriya
It is a dance form that is more than 500 years old
and the art was developed and practiced by
monks in the form dance-dramas about legends
and stories of Krishna, particularly from Bhāgavata Purāna.
